### DevOps / Platform Engineering

This role is a *systems stabilizer and flow optimizer*, sitting closer to integrators but with a narrower, operational focus.

They typically have a **moderate achievement drive**, expressed through system performance (uptime, deployment speed, reliability) rather than personal recognition.

Their **affiliation orientation is cooperative but not strongly social**: they value shared responsibility and smooth collaboration, but are less motivated by relationship-building for its own sake than integrators.

They show a strong **bias toward operational realism**:
- present vs near-future system behavior
- actual vs assumed performance
- failure modes vs intended design  

Compared to architects, they are less concerned with abstract coherence and more with **empirical system behavior over time**.

They rely on **competence-based influence**, particularly through demonstrated understanding of system dynamics and failure patterns.

In conflict, they prefer **evidence-based confrontation**, grounded in metrics, incidents, and observed behavior. They are typically impatient with purely conceptual debates detached from operational reality.

Their persistent concern is *fragility under load, scale, or change*.

**Tension with integrators:** DevOps grounds integrators’ abstractions in operational reality; conflict arises when architectural intent and system behavior diverge.
