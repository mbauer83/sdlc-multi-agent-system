### Safety / Compliance Engineer (Integrator Role)

This is perhaps the *most structurally “pure” integrator role* in modern organizations: it sits across all functions but owns none of them.

Effective individuals here have a **high affiliation need combined with independence**: they must maintain working relationships with all parties (engineering, product, legal, operations) while being willing to oppose them when necessary.

Their **achievement motivation is explicitly de-personalized**: success is defined as absence of failure (incidents, violations), which removes many conventional reward signals. This requires a stable internal orientation rather than reliance on external recognition.

They depend heavily on **credibility through expertise and judgment**, since formal authority is often limited or counterproductive. Without perceived competence, their influence collapses quickly.

They exhibit an unusually strong **balanced orientation across time horizons**:
- immediate delivery pressure vs long-term risk accumulation  
and across perspectives:
- business goals vs regulatory constraints vs technical realities  

Most importantly, they are **explicitly conflict-tolerant and confrontation-capable**. They must surface uncomfortable truths, challenge optimistic assumptions, and force consideration of low-probability/high-impact risks — often against resistance.

They neither smooth conflict away nor escalate prematurely; instead, they push toward *evidence-based resolution* focused on system safety and compliance.

Their persistent concern is *latent risk accumulation under organizational pressure to deliver*.
