### Product Owner

A strong product owner is a *value integrator*, but with a fundamentally different center of gravity than architects: they integrate around **external impact**, not internal coherence.

They typically have a **moderately high achievement drive**, oriented toward visible outcomes (usage, revenue, adoption), combined with a **pragmatic affiliation orientation**: enough to align stakeholders, but not so high that they avoid hard prioritization decisions.

Unlike architects, they are comfortable with **asymmetric trade-offs** — sacrificing internal elegance or completeness if it increases delivered value. This makes them more tolerant of inconsistency, but also more vulnerable to short-termism.

Their cognitive style is **selectively unbalanced by design**:
- strongly biased toward *user and market perspective*
- moderately attentive to delivery constraints
- only indirectly concerned with deep technical structure  

They rely on **influence through narrative and prioritization authority**, rather than deep cross-domain competence. Credibility comes from judgment about “what matters,” not from technical or operational expertise.

They are **conflict-capable but goal-directed in conflict**: they will surface disagreements when needed, but tend to resolve them by forcing prioritization decisions rather than fully reconciling perspectives.

Their persistent concern is *opportunity cost and misallocated effort* — doing the wrong thing, not doing things imperfectly.

**Tension with integrators:** product owners push for *value now*, while architects and safety roles push for *coherence and risk control over time*.
