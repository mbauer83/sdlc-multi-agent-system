### Software Architect / Principal Engineer (Integrator Role)

In its mature form, this role combines deep technical authority with **integrative leadership grounded in competence rather than hierarchy**.

These individuals tend to have a **moderate achievement drive** — enough to maintain high standards, but not so dominant that technical decisions become ego-driven or competitive. When this balance fails, architects become either rigid gatekeepers or disengaged advisors.

They show a **selective but real affiliation orientation**: while not necessarily socially driven, they recognize that system integrity depends on aligning multiple actors. They invest in communication because misalignment is seen as a technical risk.

Their defining trait is a **balanced cognitive stance across abstraction levels**:
- code vs architecture
- immediate delivery vs long-term maintainability
- local optimization vs global consistency  

They are uneasy with unilateral decisions that optimize one dimension at the expense of others.

Like effective integrators, they rely on **argument, evidence, and design clarity** to influence others, not positional authority. When authority is used, it is typically as a fallback, not a primary mechanism.

They also exhibit a **willingness to engage in direct, problem-focused conflict**: design disagreements are surfaced and worked through explicitly, rather than avoided or politicized.

Their central concern is *preserving conceptual integrity in the presence of organizational fragmentation*.
