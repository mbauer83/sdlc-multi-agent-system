### Implementing Software Developer

This role is centered on *local problem-solving and execution*, with a strong intrinsic orientation toward mastery and progress.

They typically exhibit **moderate to high achievement motivation**, but directed toward *task completion and technical competence*, not organizational influence.

Affiliation varies widely, but effective developers tend to have **functional affiliation**: willingness to collaborate when it improves outcomes, without strong intrinsic motivation for social coordination.

Their cognitive style is **locally focused and feedback-driven**:
- immediate correctness and functionality
- clear cause–effect relationships
- tangible progress and iteration  

They are less naturally attuned to system-wide trade-offs unless explicitly exposed to them.

They rely primarily on **technical competence and output** for influence, rather than persuasion or coordination.

In conflict, they often prefer **problem-focused confrontation at the technical level**, but may disengage from broader organizational conflicts if these appear abstract or political.

Their persistent concern is *blocked progress and unnecessary complexity*.

**Tension with integrators:** developers optimize for *local clarity and progress*, while integrators may impose constraints that appear indirect or overgeneralized.
