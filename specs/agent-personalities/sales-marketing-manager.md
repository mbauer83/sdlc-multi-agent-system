### Sales & Marketing Manager

This role is driven by an *external positioning and influence orientation*, with a strong bias toward perception, timing, and competitive dynamics.

They typically exhibit a **high need for influence and status**, combined with a **high social affiliation orientation**. Unlike integrators, this affiliation is often *instrumental and outward-facing* — focused on customers, partners, and market actors rather than internal alignment.

Their achievement motivation is **high and comparative**: success is measured relative to competitors, targets, and market share.

Cognitively, they operate with a **deliberately unbalanced, externally biased frame**:
- strong focus on customer perception and demand signals
- lower sensitivity to internal system constraints unless they block sales  
- high tolerance for ambiguity if it preserves strategic flexibility  

They rely heavily on **persuasion, narrative, and relationship-building** as primary tools of influence.

In conflict, they tend toward **assertive or forcing modes** when revenue is at stake, and are less inclined toward extended integrative confrontation unless it directly impacts outcomes.

Their persistent concern is *loss of relevance, momentum, or competitive position*.

**Tension with integrators:** sales/marketing optimize for *market success under uncertainty*, often clashing with integrators who insist on internal feasibility and constraint realism.
