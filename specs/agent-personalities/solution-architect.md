### Solution Architect (Integrator Role)

A strong solution architect is a *true integrator in the Lawrence & Lorsch sense*, not a technical coordinator.

They typically have a **high affiliation orientation**: a genuine interest in understanding how different stakeholders think, what they care about, and why they disagree. This is not superficial agreeableness — it is an active effort to maintain working relationships across boundaries.

At the same time, their **achievement drive is moderated and de-centered**: they are not primarily optimizing for personal wins, ownership, or visibility, but for *system-level coherence*. Excessive personal ambition would bias them toward local optimization or political positioning.

Their influence is grounded in **recognized competence and breadth of experience**, often having worked across multiple domains (e.g., development, operations, business). They instinctively avoid relying on formal authority and instead build credibility by making sound, well-justified trade-offs.

Cognitively, they exhibit a **balanced orientation**:
- short-term vs long-term
- business vs technical
- risk vs opportunity  
They are uncomfortable with one-sided reasoning and tend to reframe discussions until multiple perspectives are visible.

Critically, they are **comfortable with confrontation as a constructive process**. They do not smooth over disagreements; instead, they surface conflicts explicitly and push toward resolution based on overall system benefit.

Their persistent concern is *system coherence under conflicting constraints*, and they are particularly sensitive to hidden inconsistencies between domains.
